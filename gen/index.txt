      *David> print research
      <ul>
        <li> I am broadly interested in the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computational_number_theory"> computational aspects </a> of <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abelian_variety"> abelian varieties </a> over <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Global_field"> global fields </a> in the context of the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Birch_and_Swinnerton-Dyer_conjecture"> Birch and Swinnerton-Dyer conjecture, </a> as well as in <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hasse_principle"> local-global obstructions </a> to <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rational_point"> rational points </a> on varieties via <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manin_obstruction"> cohomological gadgets </a> such as the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brauer_group"> Brauer group. </a> My PhD thesis will be on some explicit aspects of <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Special_values_of_L-functions"> special values </a> of <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hasse%E2%80%93Weil_zeta_function"> L-functions of elliptic curves </a> twisted by <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dirichlet_character"> Dirichlet characters. </a> Recently, I have been thinking of constructing databases of <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Motive_(algebraic_geometry)"> motives </a> over <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Function_field_of_an_algebraic_variety"> global function fields, </a> analogous to the <a href="https://www.lmfdb.org/"> LMFDB. </a> I am also interested in the formalisation of <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arithmetic_geometry"> arithmetic geometry </a> in <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proof_assistant"> interactive theorem provers, </a> being a pioneer in the development of the theory of elliptic curves in <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lean_(proof_assistant)"> Lean. </a> Over the next few years, I will be formalising the proofs of fundamental arithmetic results, such as the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hasse%27s_theorem_on_elliptic_curves"> Hasse--Weil bound </a> and the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mordell%E2%80%93Weil_theorem"> Mordell--Weil theorem, </a> culminating in the statements of major theorems and <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sato%E2%80%93Tate_conjecture"> open conjectures. </a> Finally, I am passionate in pedagogical aspects of undergraduate <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematics_education"> mathematics education. </a> </li>
      </ul>
